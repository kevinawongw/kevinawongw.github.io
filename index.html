About Me.

Hello! my name is Kevina Wong [she/hers]. I am a B.S. computer science major and a business minor, and I hope to use my knowledge in both to make an impact on the technology field some day.

After studying abroad for three weeks in Xi'an, China, I was immediately inspired by the universality of technology. From there, I knew that my goal in the future is to work in technology on a global scale. Furthermore, as a member of the Global Engineering Academic Program at my university, where I am able to learn about the global impacts technology has on our society — good and bad. In this program, I am also able to practice Chinese speaking skills with others in the program with the same language interest.

As a student from the University of Colorado at Boulder, I am proud to say that I am at a school that is actively trying to close the gender gap and diversity gap in engineering and science. I am fortunate to have many female peers and diverse peers to collaborate with. I am proud to say that I am an Asian-American woman in STEM, and I hope to influence others to enter the field of engineering. In the Global Engineering Program that I referred to earlier, I was also able to live among younger students in the program and mentor/guide them in their path within engineering. Whether that may be tutoring or helping them pick classes, I was able to guide them and share my experiences with them.
